Detailed Description

The Class_Potential_R2HDM class Implementation of the CP-conserving 2HDM, as given in the manual.

  • \( -L_S = u1 \Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_1 + u2 \Phi_2^\dagger \Phi_2 + \frac{L1}{2} (\Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_1)^2 + \frac{L2}{2} (\Phi_2^\dagger \Phi_2)^2 + L3 \Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_1 \Phi_2^\dagger \Phi_2 + L4 \Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_2 \Phi_2^\dagger \Phi_1 + [ \frac{RL5}{2} (\Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_2)^2 - RealMMix \Phi_1^\dagger \Phi_2 ] \)
  • \( -L_Y = \overline{U}_L V \text{diag}(y_d,y_s,y_b) D_R \Phi_d^+ + \overline{D}_L \text{diag}(y_d,y_s,y_b) D_R \Phi_d^0 + \overline{U}_L \text{diag}(y_u, y_c, y_t) U_R (\Phi_2^0)^\ast - \overline{D}_L V^\dagger \text{diag}(y_u, y_c, y_t) U_R \Phi_2^- + \overline{E}_L \text{diag}(y_e, y_\mu, y_\tau) E_R \Phi_l^0 +\overline{\nu}_L \text{diag}(y_e, y_\mu, y_\tau) E_R \Phi_l^+ + c.c. \)
  • \( L_G = (D_\mu \Phi_1)^\dagger (D^\mu \Phi_1) + (D_\mu \Phi_2)^\dagger (D^\mu \Phi_2)\)

with

  • \( \Phi_1 = \begin{pmatrix} \Phi_1^+ \\ \Phi_1^0 \end{pmatrix} = 1/\sqrt{2} \begin{pmatrix} higgsbase[0] + I*higgsbase[1] \\ higgsbase[4] + I *higgsbase[5] \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( \Phi_2 = \begin{pmatrix} \Phi_2^+ \\ \Phi_2^0 \end{pmatrix} = 1/\sqrt{2} \begin{pmatrix} higgsbase[2] + I*higgsbase[3] \\ higgsbase[6] + I *higgsbase[7] \end{pmatrix} \)
  • \( D_\mu = -I C\_{}g/2 * W_\mu^a \sigma^a -I C\_{}gs/2 B_\mu = -\frac{I}{2} \begin{pmatrix} C\_{}gs B + C\_{}g W3 & C\_{}g (W1 -I W2) \\ C\_{}g (W1 +I W2) & C\_{}gs B - C\_{}g W3 \end{pmatrix} \) with the gauge base = [W1,W2,W3,B]
  • \( \overline{U}_L = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{u}_L & \overline{c}_L & \overline{t}_L \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( U_R = \begin{pmatrix} u_R \\ c_R \\ t_R \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( \overline{D}_L = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{d}_L & \overline{s}_L & \overline{b}_L \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( D_R = \begin{pmatrix} d_R \\ s_R \\ b_R \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( \overline{E}_L = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{e}_L & \overline{\mu}_L & \overline{\tau}_L \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( E_R = \begin{pmatrix} e_R \\ \mu_R \\ \tau_R \end{pmatrix}\,, \)
  • \( \overline{\nu}_L = \begin{pmatrix} \overline{\nu}_{e,L} & \overline{\nu}_{\mu,L} & \overline{\nu}_{\tau,L} \end{pmatrix} \)

The basis of the quarks given by \( [u_R, c_R, t_R, d_R, s_R, b_R, \overline{u}_L, \overline{c}_L, \overline{t}_L, \overline{d}_L, \overline{s}_L, \overline{b}_L ] \) and for the leptons \( [\overline{e}_L, e_R, \overline{\mu}_L , \mu_R, \overline{\tau}_L, \tau_R, \overline{\nu}_{e,L}, \overline{\nu}_{\mu,L}, \overline{\nu}_{\tau,L} ] \)

The Yukawa types defined by

  • Type I : \( \Phi_d = \Phi_l = \Phi_2 \)
  • Type II: \( \Phi_d = \Phi_l = \Phi_1 \)
  • Type LS(=3): \( \Phi_d = \Phi_2\,, \Phi_l = \Phi_1 \)
  • Type FL(=4): \( \Phi_d = \Phi_1 \,,\Phi_l = \Phi_2 \)
